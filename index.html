MY FILMS
Movies aren’t just entertainment for me—they’re an experience. I even use an app to meticulously categorize everything I’ve watched, rate them, and pour my thoughts into short (or sometimes long) commentaries.

When it comes to genres, I have a soft spot for psychological horror. There’s just something exhilarating about the feeling of being utterly mind-blown by a perfectly executed plot twist. I love that sense of confusion, that moment where everything shifts, leaving me in complete awe.

Another favorite of mine? Dystopian films. I’m drawn to their complexity, the way they build futuristic worlds that feel both thrilling and unsettling. The more intricate and thought-provoking, the better—give me something that challenges my perception of reality, and I’m hooked.

Top Favorite Of All-Time
Harry Potter Movie Series

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure
Director: Chris Columbus, Alfonso Cuarón, Mike Newell, David Yates
Main Cast: Daniel Radcliffe, Emma Watson, Rupert Grint

         I have a yearly tradition of binge-watching the entire Harry Potter movie series, and I know for a fact that I will never get tired of it. No matter how many times I rewatch it, the magic always feels just as real. While watching, I can't help but wish that someday, I’d wake up to find out that I’m actually a wizard, enrolled at Hogwarts, learning spells, brewing potions, and living in a world filled with magic. The world of Harry Potter is more than just a story— it’s an escape, a place where anything feels possible, and a universe I will always go back to.

La La Land

Genre: Musical, Romance, Drama
Director: Damien Chazelle
Main Cast: Ryan Gosling, Emma Stone

         This is my canon movie. I am still devastated by the fact that they didn’t end up together. They finally achieved their dreams, but without each other. They were so perfect together, yet some things are just not meant to be.

This might as well be the definition of a perfect movie for me— the production, the cinematography, the aesthetic, everything. And of course, the musical scores— they linger in my mind long after the movie ends.

Howl's Moving Castle

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure
Director: Hayao Miyazaki
Main Cast: Chieko Baisho, Takuya Kimura, Akihiro Miwa

         I am the biggest fan of Studio Ghibli movies, and this is my top favorite. Not just because I share a similar name with the female lead, but because everything in this movie feels like a haven to me.

The odd yet mesmerizing plot, the unique flow of the story, and the overall aesthetic of this movie make it stand out from anything else I've ever watched. There’s something so magical about *Howl’s Moving Castle*— from its breathtaking visuals to its heartwarming themes of love, identity, and freedom. I could rewatch it a hundred times and still be in awe every single time.

Psychological Horror
The Call

Genre: Thriller, Mystery, Horror
Director: Lee Chung-hyun
Main Cast: Park Shin-hye, Jeon Jong-seo

          The Call is, without a doubt, a top-tier Korean thriller. From start to finish, this movie had me on edge, constantly second-guessing what would happen next. The concept of time manipulation in this film is nothing short of brilliant— the way it intertwines the past, present, and future is both mind-bending and terrifying. I love how the story showcases the impact of a single action in the past and how it can spiral into catastrophic consequences in ways that no one can predict. This movie doesn’t just play with time; it warps reality itself, making me question every possibility and outcome.

         However, if there’s one thing that still frustrates me, it’s the ending. I went through all that tension, suspense, and anxiety, only to be left shocked and unsatisfied. Even now, I find myself thinking about the final moments, wondering if there was a different way things could have played out.

Black Swan

Genre: Psychological Thriller, Drama
Director: Darren Aronofsky
Main Cast: Natalie Portman, Mila Kunis, Vincent Cassel

          Black Swan is one of those films that left a lasting impact on me. It’s haunting, mesmerizing, and deeply unsettling—a cinematic experience that pulls you into the darkest corners of the human psyche. At its core, this film is about the pursuit of perfection— a suffocating, obsessive, and ultimately self-destructive journey. Nina’s descent into madness is both terrifying and tragic, and Natalie Portman’s performance is nothing short of extraordinary. She captures every nuance of a woman trapped between expectations and reality, constantly torn apart by her own mind and the pressure that surrounds her.

         This movie perfectly encapsulates what it feels like to be a woman— constantly striving for unattainable perfection, while being shaped and pressured by the environment and the people around you. Society demands flawlessness, but at what cost? The film explores themes of identity, obsession, and the devastating effects of pushing oneself beyond the breaking point.

Pearl

Genre: Psychological Horror, Thriller
Director: Ti West
Main Cast: Mia Goth, David Corenswet, Tandi Wright

          Pearl is a brilliantly twisted character study that peels back the layers of a woman on the verge of insanity. At its core, this movie is about unfulfilled dreams and suffocating desperation. Pearl is a woman who craves the spotlight, who dreams of escaping her mundane existence and becoming a star.

          This movie is more than just a horror movie—it’s a film about the fragile line between ambition and insanity. It explores what happens when someone is denied their dream for too long, when their longing for recognition turns into something dark and violent. The cinematography, the bright and colorful aesthetic that contrasts with the horrific themes, and the vintage Hollywood feel all make this film uniquely captivating.

Dystopian
Hunger Games

Genre: Dystopian, Action, Sci-Fi
Director: Gary Ross, Francis Lawrence
Main Cast: Jennifer Lawrence, Josh Hutcherson, Liam Hemsworth

          The Hunger Games is one of the most compelling dystopian stories ever brought to life on screen. The political undertones in The Hunger Games are eerily relevant. The cinematography and score elevate the intensity of the story. Every action scene is heart-pounding, every quiet moment filled with raw emotion. The contrast between the lavish excess of the Capitol and the harsh reality of the districts is both visually and thematically striking.

         The Hunger Games is more than just a dystopian film—it’s a social commentary. It challenges us to question authority, to recognize the dangers of power left unchecked, and to understand the true cost of war. This isn’t just about survival in the arena; it’s about the resilience of the human spirit, the will to fight against injustice, and the power of one person to change the world.

Interstellar

Genre: Sci-Fi, Adventure, Drama
Director: Christopher Nolan
Main Cast: Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Chastain

          Interstellar is hands down one of the most brilliantly crafted sci-fi films ever made. It’s not just another space movie—it’s an emotional, thought-provoking, and scientifically accurate masterpiece that challenges our perception of time, space, and human survival. I was completely hooked from start to finish, but at the same time, this movie made me feel completely dumb at certain points— because it deals with theories that are far beyond what we can comprehend.

         But beyond the science, *Interstellar* is deeply personal. At its core, it’s a story about love, sacrifice, and time. The father-daughter relationship between Cooper and Murph is one of the most heartbreaking yet powerful dynamics in the film. Few films have ever impacted me this deeply. If I could erase my memory and watch it for the first time again, I would.

Maze Runner

Genre: Sci-Fi, Action, Adventure
Director: Wes Ball
Main Cast: Dylan O'Brien, Kaya Scodelario, Thomas Brodie-Sangster

         Now *this* is what I’m talking about. A dystopian movie that had me completely hooked from the very beginning. I still remember the first time watching Maze Runner— the confusion, the thrill, the non-stop action— and just being so immersed in this chaotic world. What makes Maze Runner so good is that it doesn’t just throw action at you— it gives you something to *think* about. The deeper I got into the story, the more I realized that escaping the maze wasn’t even the worst part. The real*horror was what waited beyond it. WCKD, the experiments, the idea that survival itself is part of a bigger test— it was so unsettling but also *so addicting*.

          Watching *Maze Runner* made me wish I could experience it for the first time all over again. The twists, the suspense, the adrenaline rush—it’s exactly the kind of dystopian story that keeps me coming back for more. If I ever wake up in a Glade surrounded by a maze, though? I’d be the first to die. No question.

Whisper Of The Heart

Genre: Romance, Slice of Life, Coming-of-Age
Director: Yoshifumi Kondō
Main Cast: Yōko Honna, Issei Takahashi

         As I’ve said before, I am the *biggest* Studio Ghibli enthusiast, and Whisper of the Heart holds a very special place in my heart. This is my second favorite Ghibli film, and honestly, it’s so close to being my first. There’s just something about this movie that feels like a warm hug— the youthful energy, the small yet *profound* moments, and the kind of romance that isn’t just about love, but about growth, passion, and self-discovery.

         I have always been a huge fan of nostalgic and coming-of-age themes in movies, and this one delivers it so perfectly. There’s no dramatic conflict, no over-the-top twists— just a beautifully simple story about two young people finding themselves and each other in the midst of life’s uncertainties. No matter how many times I rewatch it, it still makes me feel the same way— like I’m young again, dreaming big, and believing that anything is possible.

Tangled

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure, Romance
Director: Nathan Greno, Byron Howard
Main Cast: Mandy Moore, Zachary Levi

         Out of all the Disney princess movies, Tangled is hands down my favorite. There’s just something so magical about it— the adventure, the humor, the romance, and of course, the lantern scene. That scene alone is enough to make this my ultimate favorite.

         At its core, Tangled is about dreams, love, and freedo*. It’s about taking that leap of faith, stepping into the unknown, and realizing that you are more capable than you think. No matter how many times I rewatch this movie, it still gives me the same warm, magical feeling. And every time I see lanterns in the internet, I can’t help but think of Rapunzel’s dream finally coming true.

Parasite

Genre: Thriller, Drama, Dark Comedy
Director: Bong Joon-ho
Main Cast: Song Kang-ho, Lee Sun-kyun, Cho Yeo-jeong, Choi Woo-shik, Park So-dam

         There’s a reason why Parasite is considered one of the greatest films of all time— and why it easily made the list of my favorite movies. This film is pure cinematic genius. Every single detail—the setting, the house, the cinematography, the way the scenes unfold—everything means something. What makes Parasite so mind-blowing is its ability to shift tones seamlessly. It starts as a dark comedy, then turns into an intense thriller.

And let’s talk about the symbolism. The film constantly plays with the idea of high and low, from the literal height difference between the rich and poor homes, to the way the characters move within the house. A film that lingers in your mind long after the credits roll. It’s a reminder that cinema isn’t just about storytelling, but about crafting something layered, meaningful, and unforgettable.
